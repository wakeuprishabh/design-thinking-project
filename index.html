Rethinking Social Media: A Design Thinking Approach

Tracing the roots of digital exhaustion, distraction, and the loss of safety.

Explore Project

Mission

Our mission is to explore the ethical and emotional impact of social media platforms and AI-driven systems on users. Through empathy-based research, we aim to design transparent, human-centered, and ethical digital experiences that promote trust, safety, and digital well-being.

Problem Statement Canvas

Using the integrated design thinking framework, we mapped our user, their context, pain points, root causes, and the scale of impact to arrive at a clear, actionable problem definition.

USER / PROTAGONIST

  • Young adults aged 18–25
  • Heavy social media users
  • Platforms: Instagram, X/Twitter, TikTok, WhatsApp, YouTube
  • Consume news quickly, struggle to verify before sharing

CONTEXT & SITUATION

  • Viral or trending posts
  • Breaking news events
  • Emotionally charged headlines
  • AI-generated videos, images, text
  • Peer pressure to stay updated
  • Fast-paced digital environment

EVIDENCE BASE

  • Users trust viral posts due to share count
  • Most do not verify before reposting
  • Fact-checking seen as time-consuming
  • AI-generated content harder to detect
  • Empathy mapping & user journey research

PAIN POINTS & NEEDS

  • Difficulty identifying trustworthy information
  • Lack of quick verification tools
  • AI-generated media increasingly realistic
  • Information overload from multiple platforms
  • Fear of sharing fake information publicly

ROOT CAUSES (5 WHYS)

  • Social media prioritizes engagement over accuracy
  • Users consume information too quickly
  • Fact-checking tools are disconnected from browsing
  • Emotional headlines encourage impulsive reactions
  • Social pressure encourages rapid reposting

IMPACT & SCALE

  • Rapid spread of misinformation
  • Public confusion and panic
  • Manipulation of opinions
  • Loss of trust in news
  • Damage to personal credibility
  • Increased polarization and distrust

Point of View Statement:

A social media user who relies on online platforms for news needs to have a fast, simple, and trustworthy way to verify information before sharing it because they want to stay informed quickly without accidentally spreading misinformation or damaging their credibility.

Research

We conducted anonymous interviews with social media users across different age groups to understand emotional, ethical, and behavioral challenges related to digital platforms.

  • Increased stress and anxiety from comparison culture
  • Difficulty identifying misinformation and fake content
  • Fear of missing out and addiction to notifications
  • Concerns about privacy and digital safety
  • Emotional exhaustion from constant online engagement

Reflections

The research revealed that social media problems are not only technological but deeply emotional and psychological.


Users desire transparency, healthier engagement systems, emotional safety, and better tools for identifying misinformation.


Ethical digital experiences should prioritize user well-being over engagement metrics.

How Might We?

HMW 01

How might we help users identify biased or manipulative content on social media platforms?

HMW 02

How might we reduce emotional overload caused by algorithmic engagement systems?

HMW 03

How might we create transparent systems that reveal how user data is being collected and used?

Ideation

Bias Radar

Detects algorithmic narrowing and encourages exposure to diverse viewpoints.

Emotional Impact Indicator

Warns users when content is emotionally manipulative or designed for outrage.

Live Data Mirror

Shows users what AI systems infer about them based on their digital behavior.

Solution

Our proposed solution combines transparency tools, emotional well-being indicators, and misinformation detection systems into a user-centered digital safety platform.

  • Identify misinformation and AI-generated content
  • Understand emotional manipulation in feeds
  • Monitor how personal data is being inferred
  • Build healthier digital engagement habits
