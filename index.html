Project Overview

This project focuses on understanding the problems faced by users while interacting with social media platforms and AI-driven systems.


Through anonymous interviews and empathy mapping, we analyzed how misinformation, comparison culture, emotional manipulation, privacy concerns, and AI-generated content affect users emotionally, mentally, and socially.


Our goal is to design ethical, transparent, and human-centered digital solutions that improve trust, safety, and accountability online.

Problems Identified

Algorithmic Echo-Chamber

Social media algorithms prioritize engagement over truth, limiting perspectives and spreading misinformation.

Deepfake & Synthetic Media

Users unknowingly share AI-generated fake media that spreads misinformation rapidly before verification.

AI Transparency Gap

Students increasingly rely on AI tools without understanding the learning process or ethical implications.

Invisible Data Harvest

Platforms collect and infer behavioral data without users fully understanding how they are being profiled.

Attention Economy Exploitation

Platforms amplify emotionally triggering content to maximize engagement and screen time.

Synthetic Identity Crisis

AI-generated personas manipulate online communities while hiding behind fake identities.

Proposed Solutions

Bias Radar

A smart social feed assistant that alerts users when algorithms narrow perspectives or reinforce bias.

Verification-First Plugin

Browser extension that flags unlabeled AI-generated content before users share it online.

Live Data Mirror

Real-time transparency tool showing users what AI systems infer about them from online activity.

Digital Authenticity Seal

Verification system for academic and institutional media to prevent fake recordings and misinformation.

Emotional Impact Indicator

Detects emotionally manipulative content designed for outrage rather than useful information.

Proof-of-Personhood

Human verification framework that confirms authenticity without exposing personal private data.
